What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers impersonate trusted people, companies, or organizations to trick users into clicking links, opening attachments, or sharing sensitive information.

  • They may try to steal passwords.
  • They may trick users into downloading malware.
  • They may request money or sensitive information.
  • They often use urgency, fear, or curiosity.

Common Warning Signs

  • Unexpected emails asking for account action
  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Suspicious links
  • Unknown sender addresses
  • Attachments you were not expecting
  • Requests for passwords or personal information
Tip: Hover over links before clicking and verify requests through official channels.

What Should You Do?

  • Slow down before clicking.
  • Check the sender carefully.
  • Do not enter passwords from email links.
  • Report suspicious emails to IT or your instructor.
  • Use multi-factor authentication whenever possible.
